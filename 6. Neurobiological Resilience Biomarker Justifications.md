# Neurobiological Resilience Biomarker Justifications

The table below describes each biomarker included in the Neurological Resilience Score, its desired direction of change, its scoring role within the methodology, its assigned weight, and the literature basis for its inclusion. Biomarkers are grouped by data source.

Scoring role refers to how each marker enters the composite score:
- **Individual (crew-relative)** — scored by deviation from the crew median using a robust z-score; these markers vary per crew member and drive individual differentiation.
- **Cohort modifier** — scored against fixed logFC thresholds and applied as a shared multiplier across all crew members; these markers come from cohort-wide assays that do not produce per-crew values.

## Urine Inflammation Panel — Individual (Crew-Relative) Scoring

| Biomarker | Desired Direction | Scoring Role | Suggested Weight | Literature-Based Justification |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| GFAP (Glial Fibrillary Acidic Protein) | Lower post/pre ratio is better | Individual (crew-relative) | 8 | GFAP is a structural cytoskeletal protein expressed almost exclusively in astrocytes of the central nervous system. Urinary GFAP elevation following physiological stress reflects astrocyte activation, reactive gliosis, or frank glial injury, each of which represents a departure from neurological homeostasis. It has been validated as a fluid biomarker of CNS injury across traumatic brain injury, neurodegeneration, and inflammatory demyelination cohorts. Sustained post-flight elevation relative to crewmates indicates disproportionate glial stress burden in that individual. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33568819/ |
| CXCL10 (IP-10 / Interferon gamma-induced protein 10) | Lower post/pre ratio is better | Individual (crew-relative) | 6 | CXCL10 is a CXC-family chemokine produced by astrocytes, microglia, and endothelial cells in response to interferon-γ signaling and is a well-established mediator of neuroinflammatory recruitment. Elevated urinary CXCL10 has been documented in multiple sclerosis, viral encephalitis, and systemic inflammatory states that carry CNS consequence. In the spaceflight context, CXCL10 elevation signals an ongoing neuroimmune response that is not resolved post-flight. Persistent post-flight elevation above crew norms indicates a neuroinflammatory state that has not resolved to crew-typical levels. https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/immunology/articles/10.3389/fimmu.2019.01295/full |
| BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) | Higher post/pre ratio is better | Individual (crew-relative) | 5 | BDNF is the most abundantly expressed neurotrophin in the adult brain and is essential for synaptic plasticity, long-term potentiation, and neuronal survival across hippocampal and prefrontal circuits. Peripheral BDNF, while partially confounded by platelet release, is correlated with central BDNF tone in longitudinal studies and is depressed in major depression, cognitive decline, and chronic stress. Suppression of BDNF relative to crewmates suggests inadequate neurotrophic support and impaired synaptic maintenance in the recovery phase. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30683798/ |
| NGF (Nerve Growth Factor) | Higher post/pre ratio is better | Individual (crew-relative) | 4 | NGF is the founding member of the neurotrophin family and supports the survival, maintenance, and function of sympathetic and sensory neurons as well as cholinergic neurons of the basal forebrain, which are critically involved in attention and memory. Urinary NGF reflects peripheral nervous system trophic status and is reduced in peripheral neuropathy, autonomic dysfunction, and states of chronic physiological stress. Post-flight suppression relative to crew norms suggests impaired peripheral neural maintenance that may accompany or presage broader neurological compromise. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12823469/ |

## Plasma Proteomics — Cohort Modifier

| Biomarker | Desired Direction | Scoring Role | Suggested Weight | Literature-Based Justification |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| BDNF (Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor) | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 8 | Plasma BDNF logFC reflects the cohort-wide shift in neurotrophic support across the mission. BDNF upregulation at the proteome level indicates preserved or enhanced neuroprotective signaling in response to the spaceflight environment, which is consistent with adaptive synaptic remodeling. Suppression of cohort-wide BDNF expression indicates that the entire crew has entered a state of reduced neurotrophic support, which serves as an adverse modifier for all individual scores. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30683798/ |
| S100B | Lower logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 7 | S100B is a calcium-binding protein secreted by astrocytes and Schwann cells whose plasma elevation is a well-validated indicator of blood-brain barrier disruption and glial stress. It is routinely used clinically as an acute CNS injury biomarker and is elevated in traumatic brain injury, stroke, and hypoxic encephalopathy. Cohort-wide upregulation of S100B indicates that the spaceflight environment has imposed generalized astrocyte stress across all crew members, representing an adverse condition that modulates each individual's resilience score downward. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15082000/ |
| NRGN (Neurogranin) | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 4 | Neurogranin is a postsynaptic calmodulin-binding protein expressed predominantly in dendritic spines of pyramidal neurons in the hippocampus and cortex. It plays a direct role in long-term potentiation and is regarded as a biomarker of synaptic integrity; its loss in CSF and plasma is associated with Alzheimer's disease, traumatic brain injury, and synaptic loss more broadly. Cohort-wide suppression of NRGN indicates mission-associated synaptic compromise that represents a negative modifier for all crew members' neurological resilience. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26601940/ |
| CLU (Clusterin) | Contextual — moderate elevation favorable | Cohort modifier | 3 | Clusterin is a multifunctional extracellular chaperone with neuroprotective roles including clearance of misfolded proteins, inhibition of complement-mediated cytotoxicity, and support of stressed cells under proteotoxic load. However, extreme upregulation of clusterin is also a feature of severe neurodegeneration and unresolved proteostatic stress, reflecting a state in which chaperone-mediated buffering is overwhelmed. A moderate logFC elevation is treated as favorable, while very large upregulation or suppression is penalized; both extremes indicate departure from adaptive neuroprotective balance. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33753155/ |
| APOE (Apolipoprotein E) | Lower logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 3 | APOE is the primary lipid transport protein in the central nervous system and plays a key role in neuronal membrane repair, myelin remodeling, and amyloid clearance. While APOE is essential at baseline levels, elevated plasma APOE in the context of physiological stress is associated with neuroinflammatory activation, disrupted lipid homeostasis, and reduced amyloid clearance efficiency. Cohort-wide upregulation of APOE in plasma signals a lipid-inflammatory stress response that represents a modest adverse modifier for all crew members. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31820734/ |

## Plasma Metabolomics — Cohort Modifier

| Biomarker | Desired Direction | Scoring Role | Suggested Weight | Literature-Based Justification |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| Kynurenine:Tryptophan Ratio (derived) | Lower ratio logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 7 | The kynurenine:tryptophan (KYN:TRP) ratio is the most widely used circulating index of indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase (IDO) pathway activation, a key mechanism by which systemic inflammation diverts tryptophan away from serotonin synthesis toward neurotoxic kynurenine metabolites including quinolinic acid. Elevated KYN:TRP is documented in major depressive disorder, cognitive impairment, chronic neuroinflammation, and long-duration spaceflight stress. Cohort-wide elevation of this ratio indicates a systemic neuroimmune shift that compromises serotonergic and neuroprotective metabolic tone across all crew members. https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fnins.2022.982537/full |
| Cortisol | Lower logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 6 | Cortisol is the primary glucocorticoid mediator of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis stress response and exerts broad suppressive effects on CNS plasticity. Chronic cortisol elevation reduces hippocampal BDNF expression, impairs long-term potentiation, promotes dendritic atrophy in the prefrontal cortex, and accelerates hippocampal volume loss. Spaceflight is a well-documented activator of the HPA axis, and cohort-wide cortisol upregulation represents a potent adverse modifier that attenuates the neurological resilience estimate for all crew members. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9531670/ |
| 5-HIAA (5-Hydroxyindoleacetic Acid) | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 5 | 5-HIAA is the primary metabolic end-product of serotonin degradation and serves as a proxy measure of serotonergic activity in the CNS. Reduced plasma and urinary 5-HIAA reflects depletion of serotonergic tone, which is associated with depression, anxiety, impaired impulse control, and disrupted sleep architecture — all domains of documented concern in long-duration spaceflight. Cohort-wide suppression of 5-HIAA indicates a mission-level reduction in serotonergic function that represents an adverse shared context for neurological resilience assessment. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/6142451/ |
| N-Acetylaspartic Acid (NAA) | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 5 | NAA is synthesized in neuronal mitochondria and is the second most abundant free amino acid in the brain after glutamate. It is a sensitive and specific marker of neuronal metabolic integrity; plasma and CSF NAA decline in response to neuronal energy failure, mitochondrial dysfunction, and early neurodegeneration, well before structural changes are detectable by imaging. Cohort-wide suppression of NAA in the metabolome indicates compromised neuronal metabolic function across the crew and represents an adverse modifier for neurological resilience estimates. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10386160/ |
| Tryptophan | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 4 | Tryptophan is the sole dietary precursor for serotonin, melatonin, and neuroprotective kynurenic acid synthesis, and its plasma availability is rate-limiting for serotonergic neurotransmission. Depletion of circulating tryptophan — common under states of systemic inflammation driven by IDO activation — reduces the substrate pool for serotonin synthesis and shifts kynurenine pathway flux toward neurotoxic rather than neuroprotective metabolites. Cohort-wide tryptophan suppression indicates a precursor-availability constraint on serotonergic neuroprotection that worsens the neurological context for all crew members. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10617990/ |
| Kynurenine | Lower logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 4 | Kynurenine is the primary immediate catabolite of tryptophan through the IDO/TDO pathway and is the precursor to both neuroprotective (kynurenic acid) and neurotoxic (quinolinic acid, 3-hydroxykynurenine) downstream metabolites. When the pathway is chronically activated by inflammation, the balance shifts toward neurotoxic products that stimulate NMDA receptors, generate oxidative stress, and promote neuronal apoptosis. Cohort-wide kynurenine elevation indicates ongoing inflammatory diversion of tryptophan toward potentially neurotoxic catabolites, representing an adverse modifier for all crew resilience scores. https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fnins.2022.982537/full |
| Nicotinamide | Higher logFC is better | Cohort modifier | 4 | Nicotinamide is a form of vitamin B3 and a direct precursor to NAD⁺, the central coenzyme in mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation and a required substrate for PARP-mediated DNA repair and SIRT1-mediated neuroprotective signaling. NAD⁺ depletion in neurons is associated with impaired mitochondrial function, increased susceptibility to excitotoxic death, and reduced resilience to oxidative stress — all relevant in the context of spaceflight radiation exposure. Cohort-wide suppression of nicotinamide indicates a reduced capacity for neuronal NAD⁺ regeneration, representing an adverse metabolic context that modulates all individual resilience scores downward. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31174325/ |
